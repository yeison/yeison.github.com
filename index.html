another list of useful unix commands

  • 'stat' or 'fstat'

A new unix command that I encountered today. Do you want more details from a file than you could ever ask for? Try running 'stat -x ' (the command may be 'fstat' depending on your flavor of *nix.

Here is some example output:

stat -x grep.c File: "grep.c" Size: 1939 FileType: Regular File Mode: (0644/-rw-r--r--) Uid: ( 501/ Yeison) Gid: ( 20/ staff) Device: 1,1 Inode: 227606880 Links: 1 Access: Wed Oct 31 21:13:50 2012 Modify: Wed Oct 31 20:38:15 2012 Change: Wed Oct 31 20:38:15 2012
